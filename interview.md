Took a GenAI interview recently.

I gave the candidate a simple scenario:

"You are building a RAG system for compliance users. The answer is correct 80% of the time, but sometimes it misses important policy context. What do you fix first?"

Most people jumped to the LLM.

Bigger model.
Better prompt.
More context.
Different temperature.

That was not the answer I was looking for.

The better answer starts before the LLM.

Check retrieval coverage.
Check chunk quality.
Check metadata filters.
Check source freshness.
Check whether the evaluation set reflects real user questions.
Check if the system knows when not to answer.

In enterprise AI, the model is only one part of the risk.

The system around the model decides whether people trust it.

A senior AI engineer should not only ask, "Can the model answer?"

They should ask, "Can the business safely depend on this answer?"

What would you check first in this scenario?
